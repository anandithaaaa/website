Community Health Worker Led, AI-enabled Care in Nepal

Smartphone-Based Blood Clotting Detection

Developed a contactless system using smartphone LiDAR sensors to assess blood coagulation. By directing a coherent laser at a blood droplet and analyzing the resulting speckle pattern's Brownian motion, the system distinguishes between coagulated and uncoagulated samples. This approach requires no hardware attachments and could enable community health workers to assess clotting status using existing smartphones.

AI-Powered Atrial Fibrillation Detection

Recognizing that expert cardiologists and 12-lead ECG machines are unavailable in regions like Nepal with high cardiovascular disease burden, this research uses mobile 6-lead ECG devices with AI models. The system predicts whether a patient will experience atrial fibrillation within a 60-day window, aligning with CHWs' typical visit schedules and exceeding traditional diagnostic capabilities.

mHealth Integration into Health Systems

This work examines how to successfully integrate diagnostic AI tools into existing country health systems by comparing government visions for such technologies against implementation realities, developing a framework to assess whether conceived solutions can meaningfully impact patient outcomes.
